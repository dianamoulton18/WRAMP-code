Introduction



A pie chart is a circular graph that is split into several slices, like a fraction, to represent a proportion in a data.

For example, the pie chart above shows a proportion of 1/4 or (25%) where 25% for the graphics is colored in darker purple.

Proportions and percentages are also used to describe chances. For example, there is 25% of chance of raining tomorrow. However, people usually use expressions like "likely", "probably" when they talk about probabilities and chances.For example, "It will likely rain tomorrow", "There is only a little chance that I'll travel to Paris again" etc...

In this study, we are interested in the words and expressions that people use to describe probabilities shown in a data visualization like a pie chart.

For this experiment, imagine yourself participating in a game where you have to pick a marble from a set at random. Some of the marbles are pink, and some of them are purple. The game has two outcomes:
- If you pull a purple marble, you win.
- If you pull a pink marble, you lose.

In the pie chart provided above, the purple section of the graph represents the proportion of purple marbles in the set. A quarter of the total number of marbles in the set is purple.
In general, the bigger the purple slice is, the more purple marbles there are in the set. Your chance of picking one and winning the game will vary accordingly.

Your task: For each question in this questionnaire, observe the visualization and describe your chance of picking a purple marble and winning the game.
Do NOT use numerical answer like "The chance of drawing a purple marble is 25%". Instead, use familiar probability words and expressions to describe and communicate your chances.

Since this is a scientific experiment, your answers could be rejected if they do not follow these instructions
